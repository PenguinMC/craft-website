The Frequency Math

2–3 lessons a week.
That’s the sweet spot.

Self-paced doesn’t mean slow. It means flexible. The pilots who finish their rating cheapest are the ones who fly often enough that nothing rusts between lessons.

Why cadence beats hours.

Every flight has a warm-up. The first 15 minutes of any lesson is re-learning what you already learned last time. The longer the gap between flights, the bigger the warm-up tax.

Fly once every two weeks and you might spend 30% of every lesson just getting back to where you were. Fly twice a week and that drops to under 10%. Fly three times and you’re building, not refreshing.

Translation: the cheapest path to the rating is the most consistent one. Hourly billing rewards frequency.

The quicker you commit up front, the cheaper it gets in the long run.

How It Works

Four steps.
From first call to first solo.

The process is intentionally simple. We meet you, match you with a CFI, get you into the portal, and start flying. The rest is showing up.

01

Reach Out

Call, email, or use the contact form. Tell us where you are: brand-new, lapsed, or partway through. We’ll set up a no-pressure walk-through at the school.

02

Get Matched

We pair you with a personal CFI based on availability, personality, and the rating you’re after. You stay with that instructor through your training — no rotation.

03

Onboard to Flight Circle

You get an account in our scheduling portal. Aircraft, instructors, sims, payments, lesson notes — all in one place. We’ll walk you through it on day one.

04

Start Flying

Coordinate with your CFI on a cadence that fits both of you. Most students settle into 2–3 lessons a week. You log it. We invoice it. You finish it.
