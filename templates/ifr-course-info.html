Why sim-first works

An IFR student's biggest enemy is repetition cost. In the airplane, one approach plus missed plus reset eats 30 minutes of Hobbs and a chunk of fuel. In the Redbird, you fly the same approach four times in 20 minutes, then hit the airplane with it dialed. The FAA lets you log a meaningful chunk of your required instrument time in the AATD.

How the week runs
  • Days 1-2: scan fundamentals, tracking, intercepting, holds, DME arcs in the sim, then fly it
  • Days 3-5: approaches, step-downs, glide slopes, unusual attitude recovery, building to full procedures
  • Day 6: mock checkride, oral prep, polish weak areas
  • Day 7: checkride. Answer the questions, fly three approaches, walk out instrument-rated

This outline is the rough draft. We tailor the week to you, not a Part 141 syllabus. That's the CRAFT advantage.
