Why train at KCHS

Class C airspace. Real ATC, real radio work, real wake separation. You learn to communicate with controllers from your first lesson. Students from outlying fields often don't get this for years.

Why the DA40 NG matters

Composite airframe, Jet-A FADEC engine, Garmin G1000 NXi glass cockpit with synthetic vision, GFC700 digital autopilot. Most schools still train on 1970s panels that don't transfer to airline equipment. Every hour you log on a G1000 platform is an hour you don't relearn when you upgrade to IFR, commercial, or your first type rating.

About the hours

The FAA minimum is 40 hours. Where you actually land depends on training cadence, weather, and aptitude. We build every estimate at the 40-hour mark so you know your floor, and the cost calculator on our site gives you a real number based on your goals.

Checkride, handled

We work directly with local DPEs to schedule your checkride. No waitlist scramble. When you're ready, the date is locked.
