to airline cockpit.

Most people who walk in have never touched a plane. That’s normal — everyone starts at zero. Hover (or tap on mobile) any stop below to see what that rating actually is, what it lets you do, and how long it takes.

Glossary · Quick definitions
FBO — the company that runs the building you walk into at the airport. We operate at Charleston International (KCHS).
DPE — the FAA examiner who gives you your final practical test (a.k.a. checkride) at the end of each rating. CRAFT’s DPE waits run in weeks, not months.
Checkride — the final FAA test for each rating. Oral exam + flight test, ~3 hours total.
BYOP — “Bring Your Own Plane.” If you own an aircraft, accelerated programs cost less because you’re not renting ours.

The Sim

Master procedures on the ground.

The Redbird AATD is the same FAA-approved AATD the airlines use. We're the flight school in Charleston that owns and operates one in-house — and it's how our students hit checkride minimums with real margin.

  • 20 sim hours count toward IFR training and lock in flows before they cost you airplane time.
  • True-to-aircraft realism with weather, IMC, and emergency scenarios you can't legally practice in the airplane.
  • $100/hr sim time — the smartest hour in your logbook before you ever burn fuel.
  • Available for rated pilots for currency, IPC prep, and type-rating prep — not just students.
