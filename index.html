Our Wedge

The bottleneck stops here.

The biggest reason "accelerated" training breaks down elsewhere isn't instruction — it's checkrides. Across most of the country, pilots wait 4–6 months for a Designated Pilot Examiner.

At CRAFT, DPE availability is built into how every program is planned. While exact timing varies, checkrides are typically scheduled in weeks, not months. You train, you test, you finish.

The Sim

Master procedures on the ground.

The Redbird FMX is the same FAA-approved AATD the airlines use. We're the only flight school in Charleston that owns and operates one in-house — and it's how our students hit checkride minimums with real margin.

  • 20 sim hours count toward IFR training and lock in flows before they cost you airplane time.
  • True-to-aircraft realism with weather, IMC, and emergency scenarios you can't legally practice in the airplane.
  • $70/hr sim time — the smartest hour in your logbook before you ever burn fuel.
  • Available for rated pilots for currency, IPC prep, and type-rating prep — not just students.
