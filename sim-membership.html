▸ Why train in the sim

The math is undefeated.

Every hour in the sim is an hour you didn't burn $185+ on the meter. Combine that with FAA-loggable AATD time toward your rating and the ability to repeat one approach 12 times until it's automatic — and the sim becomes the cheat code most flight schools won't tell you about.

  • $70/hr in the sim vs. $255/hr in the DA40NG. Burn $740 instead of $1,540 to nail down a checkride maneuver. Your wallet thanks you.
  • Up to 20 hours loggable toward your IFR rating. The Redbird FMX is FAA AATD-certified — that time counts on your 8710.
  • Pause, freeze, repeat. Lost the localizer 3 miles out? Reset and try again 30 seconds later. You can't do that in the airplane.
  • Weather no-go = sim go. Charleston gets thunderstorms in summer and gusty fronts in winter. The sim runs in any weather.
  • Real instrument experience without the pressure. Fly an ILS to minimums, lose an engine, divert to an alternate — without the consequences. Build the muscle memory before the airplane needs it.

▸ The AATD difference

Not just a video game.

FACT 01

FAA AATD-Certified

The Redbird FMX is approved by the FAA as an Advanced Aviation Training Device under §61.65. That means time logged in it counts toward your IFR rating — up to 20 hours.

FACT 02

Full-Motion Cockpit

Three-axis motion platform. The cockpit physically pitches, rolls, and yaws to match your control inputs. Your inner ear gets the same cues it would in the airplane.

FACT 03

Realistic Avionics

Configurable as G1000, Garmin GTN, or steam gauges. Train in the same panel you'll fly — including DA40 NG and DA42-VI configurations.

FACT 04

Real Weather Modeling

Live weather injection from real METARs. Practice icing, gusty crosswinds, low ceilings, and convective activity — all without leaving the ground.

FACT 05

Approach Library

Every published instrument approach in the U.S. is in the database. Run an ILS to KSAV, an RNAV to KCHS, or a circle-to-land at KMYR — all in one session.

FACT 06

Same Tech as the Airlines

Major regional carriers and Part 141 schools use the same Redbird FMX platform. If it's good enough for SkyWest CFIs, it's good enough for your IFR prep.
